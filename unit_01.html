Core Reading: Human Connections

Part 1: The Social Circle

Relationships are the foundation of our daily lives. Most people have a close-knit group of family and friends. Take Maya and Laura, for instance; they are colleagues who often meet for coffee. Maya is currently waiting for her friend Anna, an exchange student from Poland who is studying here.

Part 2: Nature vs. Nurture

But what defines our character? Scientists often debate whether it is our genes or our childhood. Some argue that our biology—the DNA we inherit from our parents—determines if we are patient, strict, or bright. Others believe that our environment, such as how our parents treated us and who our friends were, forms our personality.

Part 3: The Art of Communication

To maintain these bonds, we must communicate. Humans are the great communicators; we have developed about 6,000 languages to exchange complex ideas. However, we aren't alone. Animals exchange information too—bees dance to show where food is, elephants use low sounds, and whales sing. Yet, human language remains unique in its ability to discuss the past, present, and future intentions.
